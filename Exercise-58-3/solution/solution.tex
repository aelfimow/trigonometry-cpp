\section{Exercise 58.2}

$f(x) = 2 \cos^{2}{A} - 1$\\
\\
$\sin^{2}{A} + \cos^{2}{A} = 1$\\
\\
$f(x) = 2 \cos^{2}{A} - (\sin^{2}{A} + \cos^{2}{A})$\\
$f(x) = 2 \cos^{2}{A} - \sin^{2}{A} - \cos^{2}{A}$\\
$f(x) = \cos^{2}{A} - \sin^{2}{A}$\\
\\
Solution:\\
$2 \cos^{2}{A} - 1 = \cos^{2}{A} - \sin^{2}{A}$\\
